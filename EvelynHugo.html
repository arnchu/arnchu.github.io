The Seven husbands of evelyn hugo

“I know the whole world prefers a woman who doesn’t know her power, but I’m sick of all that.”

SYNOPSIS: Aging and reclusive Hollywood movie icon Evelyn Hugo is finally ready to tell the truth about her glamorous and scandalous life. But when she chooses magazine reporter Monique Grant for the job, no one is more astounded than Monique herself. Why her? Why now? Monique is not exactly on top of the world. Her husband has left her, and her professional life is going nowhere. Regardless of why Evelyn has selected her to write her biography, Monique is determined to use this opportunity to jumpstart her career. Summoned to Evelyn’s luxurious apartment, Monique listens in fascination as the actress tells her story. From making her way to Los Angeles in the 1950s to her decision to leave show business in ‘80s, and of course, the seven husbands along the way, Evelyn unspools a tale of ruthless ambition, unexpected friendship, and a great, forbidden love. Monique begins to feel a very real connection to the legendary star, but as Evelyn’s story nears its conclusion, it becomes clear that her life intersects with Monique’s own in tragic and irreversible ways. Written with Reid’s signature talent for creating “complex, likeable characters” (Real Simple), this is a mesmerising journey through the splendor of old Hollywood into the harsh realities of the present day as two women struggle with what it means – and costs – to face the truth.

My review : The Seven Husbands of Evelyn Hugo is not the sort of book that I would normally read and yet, it is undoubtedly the best book I’ve read in recent memory. Sold as a historical romance, the book follows the spellbinding tale of Evelyn Hugo, a young actress, who is determined to leave her mark during the Golden age of Hollywood. Armed with her boundless ambition and ability to captivate, Evelyn is unapologetic about doing whatever it takes reach the top. Make no mistake, this is not the tragic story of a sultry actress burdened by fame but, rather a celebration of what it means to be human.

Evelyn Hugo is one of the most richly nuanced characters that I have ever encountered; one moment she is ruthless, scheming and cold-hearted and the next she is compassionate and oh, so painfully human. Beneath the guise of fame and success, her story transcends time and resonates with all of us as we see a bit of ourselves in her struggles and triumphs. The Seven Husbands of Evelyn Hugo also serves as a powerful exploration of race, sexuality, misogyny and the pressure to conform to societal standards. Upon finishing this book, I felt reflective, wistful and touched by how many things can happen in life that are both awful and wondrous. This book was also a reminder that life is too short to pretend to be someone we’re not and that, we shouldn’t spend it doing anything less than loving the people who are deserving of our love. Overall, The Seven Husbands of Evelyn Hugo was an intoxicating read that is as beautifully constructed as it is utterly captivating.

Notable Themes and Ideas: Cautionary tale of how low can be situational and how quickly relationships can become abusive. The notion that friendship and companionship is just as valid as marraige.

Rating: 10/10 This book made me question some of my values and challenged my perception of the world.
